About Me

I am a Staff Researcher / Principal Engineer at Huawei Technologies Canada's Smart Grid Technology Lab, where I lead the Microgrid & AI team. My research focuses on developing advanced microgrid solutions with interpretable data-driven intelligence to address safety- and time-critical challenges in modern power systems. Before joining Huawei, I was a postdoctoral researcher at McGill University, under supervision of Prof. Xiaozhe Wang and Prof. Geza Joos, exploiting the state-of-the-art synchrophasor and smart sensor techniques to develop novel data-driven control algorithms that can alleviate or avoid critical grid situations in real-time operations.

The rapid integration of renewable energy sources poses significant challenges to grid operation safety and optimality, especially under various types of severe grid conditions and events. My work aims to bridge this gap through innovative approaches combining artificial intelligence, control theory, and power systems engineering.
