About Me

I am a Staff Researcher and Principal Engineer at Huawei Technologies Canada's Smart Grid Technology Lab, where I lead the Microgrid & AI team. My research focuses on developing advanced microgrid solutions with interpretable data-driven intelligence to address safety- and time-critical challenges in modern power systems.

The rapid integration of renewable energy sources poses significant challenges to grid operation safety and optimality, especially under various types of severe grid conditions and events. My work aims to bridge this gap through innovative approaches combining artificial intelligence, control theory, and power systems engineering.

Research Interests

My research interests lie at the intersection of power systems, artificial intelligence, and control engineering:

  • Data-Driven Modeling & Control: Physics-informed machine learning, Koopman-based methods for microgrid stability
  • Microgrid Systems: Autonomous control, energy management systems (EMS), digital twins
  • Distributed Energy Resource Integration: Grid situational awareness, forecasting, resilience enhancement, ICT interoperability and function adaptivity
  • Demand-Side Management: Aggregated load forecasting and control, thermostatically controlled loads (TCLs)
  • AI for Smart Grids: Deep learning for power systems, intelligent decision-making algorithms
